Weather, Policy, and Topic Modeling of Homelessness‑Related 311 Reports in San Diego

Exploring how environmental factors and city policy influence the spatial and thematic patterns of homelessness in San Diego using data‑driven topic modeling and spatial analysis.

Problem Statement

San Diego has seen ongoing challenges related to homelessness, with encampments shifting dynamically across urban areas. While both weather variability and policy enforcement are thought to influence these movements, quantitative evidence remains limited.

This project leverages topic modeling of homelessness‑related 311 service requests to uncover recurring themes in public reporting and connect them with spatial encampment migration, local weather patterns, and policy actions. Our objective is to provide data‑driven insight into the intersection of environmental conditions, governance, and the lived realities of unhoused populations.
